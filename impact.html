The Cultural Impact of JoJo's Bizarre Adventure

Since its debut in 1987, JoJo's Bizarre Adventure has had a massive influence on Japanese popular culture and beyond. The series' unique aesthetic, memorable characters, and quotable lines have permeated various media forms and have been referenced countless times in anime, manga, video games, and more.

Iconic Phrases and Memes

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure has given birth to numerous catchphrases and expressions that have become integral parts of internet culture. Here are some of the most famous ones:

  • "Yare Yare Daze" - Jotaro Kujo's signature phrase, roughly translating to "Good Grief" or "Give me a break."
  • "MUDA MUDA MUDA MUDA!" - Dio Brando's battle cry, meaning "Useless!"
  • "ORA ORA ORA ORA!" - The battle cry of Star Platinum, Jotaro's Stand.
  • "ZA WARUDO!" - Dio's exclamation when activating his Stand's time-stopping ability, "The World."
  • "WRYYYYYY!" - A vampiric screech associated with Dio.
  • "This must be the work of an enemy Stand!" - A phrase often used by characters when encountering strange phenomena.
  • "I, Giorno Giovanna, have a dream." - Giorno's famous declaration of his ambition.
  • "KONO DIO DA!" - "It was me, Dio!" A memorable line from the first episode that became one of the earliest JoJo memes.

References in Other Media

Gintama frequently parodies JoJo's distinctive art style and poses.

Amazing world of Gumball contains several visual references to JoJo.

The video game League of Legends has a character pose that directly references JoJo.

The Persona series of video games draws heavy inspiration from JoJo's concept of Stands.

Even South Park has included a scene that parodies JoJo's iconic art style and dramatic confrontations.

Influence on Fashion

Hirohiko Araki's love for high fashion is evident throughout JoJo's Bizarre Adventure. The characters' distinctive, flamboyant outfits and poses have inspired real-world fashion:

  • Gucci collaborated with Araki on a fashion line and a manga called "Rohan Kishibe Goes to Gucci."
  • The fashion brands Balenciaga and JW Anderson have produced collections that seem influenced by JoJo's aesthetics.
  • Numerous fashion enthusiasts and cosplayers attempt to recreate the unique style of JoJo characters.
  • The series' emphasis on striking poses has influenced photography and modeling.

Musical Connections

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure is famous for its musical references. Araki named many characters and Stands after musicians, bands, and songs, particularly from Western rock and pop:

  • Characters named after bands/musicians: Robert E.O. Speedwagon (REO Speedwagon), Will A. Zeppeli (Led Zeppelin), Dio Brando (Ronnie James Dio)
  • Stands named after songs/albums: Crazy Diamond (Shine On You Crazy Diamond by Pink Floyd), Killer Queen (by Queen), King Crimson (the band)
  • The ending theme for the first season of the anime adaptation, "Roundabout" by Yes, became a meme in itself with the "To Be Continued" arrow.
