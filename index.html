What is JoJo's Bizarre Adventure?

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure (Japanese: ジョジョの奇妙な冒険, Hepburn: JoJo no Kimyō na Bōken) is a Japanese manga series written and illustrated by Hirohiko Araki. It was originally serialized in Weekly Shōnen Jump from 1987 to 2004, and was transferred to the monthly seinen manga magazine Ultra Jump in 2005. The series is divided into nine story arcs, each following a new protagonist bearing the "JoJo" nickname.

The series is famous for its iconic art style, creative battle sequences, memorable characters, and its absolutely bizarre storylines that range from vampire hunting in Victorian England to Italian gangsters with supernatural powers.

The Parts of JoJo's Bizarre Adventure

Part 1: Phantom Blood

Set in Victorian England, Jonathan Joestar battles his adopted brother, the vampiric Dio Brando, using an ancient martial art called Hamon.

Part 2: Battle Tendency

Joseph Joestar, Jonathan's grandson, fights against the ancient Pillar Men who created the Stone Mask that transformed Dio into a vampire.

Part 3: Stardust Crusaders

Jotaro Kujo travels to Egypt to save his mother from the resurrected Dio. This part introduces "Stands," manifestations of psychic power that become the primary supernatural element in the series.

Part 4: Diamond is Unbreakable

Josuke Higashikata, Joseph's illegitimate son, protects the town of Morioh from various Stand users, including a serial killer with a Stand that can create explosions.

Part 5: Golden Wind

Giorno Giovanna, the son of Dio, joins a mafia organization in Italy with the goal of becoming a "Gang-Star" and reforming the gang from within.

Araki's Unique Art Style

One of the most distinctive features of JoJo's Bizarre Adventure is Hirohiko Araki's evolving art style. From the muscular heroes of the early parts to the more fashion-forward and slender designs in later arcs, Araki's love for high fashion and classical art is evident throughout the series.

Araki is famous for his dramatic character poses, which have become iconic in pop culture. These "JoJo poses" are often imitated by fans and referenced in other media, and have become a signature element of the series.
