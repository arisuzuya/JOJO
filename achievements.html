Recognition and Achievements

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure is one of the longest-running and most successful manga series of all time. Created by Hirohiko Araki, the series has achieved numerous milestones since its debut in 1987. Here's a look at some of JoJo's most significant achievements and recognitions.

Sales and Publication Records

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure has achieved remarkable success in terms of sales and longevity:

  • The manga has sold over 120 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling manga series of all time.
  • It has been running for over 35 years, spanning more than 130 volumes across its various parts.
  • The series has been translated into multiple languages and published internationally.
  • In 2018, JoJo's Bizarre Adventure celebrated its 30th anniversary with special exhibitions and events throughout Japan.

The extensive collection of JoJo's Bizarre Adventure manga volumes

Exhibition at the Louvre

One of the most prestigious recognitions for JoJo's Bizarre Adventure came in 2009 when Hirohiko Araki was invited to exhibit his artwork at the Louvre Museum in Paris, France.

Araki became the second manga artist ever to be featured at the world-famous museum, following in the footsteps of Naoki Urasawa. For the exhibition, Araki created a special one-shot manga titled "Rohan at the Louvre," featuring Rohan Kishibe, a character from Part 4: Diamond is Unbreakable.

This achievement represents a significant milestone not just for JoJo's Bizarre Adventure, but for manga as an art form. It demonstrated that manga could be recognized and appreciated as a legitimate artistic medium by one of the world's most prestigious cultural institutions.

Cover art for "Rohan at the Louvre," the special one-shot created for the Louvre exhibition

Critical Acclaim and Awards

Manga Awards

The series has received numerous nominations and awards throughout its run, including recognition at the Manga Awards and the Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize.

Anime Recognition

The anime adaptation has been critically acclaimed, with several nominations for Anime of the Year and Best Animation at various awards ceremonies.

Critical Praise

JoJo's Bizarre Adventure consistently ranks high in critics' lists of the best manga and anime series of all time.

Cultural Heritage Recognition

Beyond sales and critical acclaim, JoJo's Bizarre Adventure has been recognized for its cultural significance:

  • The series has been cited as influential by numerous manga artists and has inspired countless other works.
  • Locations featured in the series, particularly in Part 4: Diamond is Unbreakable, have become tourist attractions in Japan.
  • In 2012, an exhibition celebrating 25 years of JoJo was held at the Gucci Museum in Florence, Italy, further cementing the connection between the series and high fashion.
  • Character designs and poses from JoJo have been recognized as iconic in popular culture and have influenced other media beyond manga and anime.

A JoJo exhibition celebrating the series' cultural impact

Successful Spin-offs and Adaptations

The success of JoJo's Bizarre Adventure has led to numerous spin-offs and adaptations:

  • Multiple successful anime seasons covering Parts 1-5, with more in production
  • Several video games across various platforms
  • Light novels expanding the universe
  • Merchandise including figures, clothing, and collectibles
  • Spin-off manga series focusing on side characters like "Thus Spoke Kishibe Rohan"
